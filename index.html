01 — The Big Picture

Nearly 1 in 4 Ringgits Comes From the Digital Economy

Malaysia's digital economy grew to RM451.3 billion in 2024, representing 23.4% of GDP. ICT and e-commerce together now form a larger share of the economy than agriculture, construction, and mining combined. E-commerce income crossed RM1.23 trillion in 2024 — growth of 3.9% from the previous year.

Key insight: E-commerce income in Malaysia grew from RM289.4 billion in Q1 2023 to RM313.8 billion in Q4 2024 — a 8.4% rise in just two years, driven by both business and consumer transactions.

02 — Connected Nation

Where You Live Determines How Connected You Are

Nationally, 96.8% of Malaysian households have internet access — but this national figure hides dramatic state-level differences. Sabah and Sarawak lag nearly 6–9 percentage points behind the national leaders, and their rural areas face access rates as low as 80%. The digital divide is not just urban vs rural — it is also West vs East Malaysia.

Key insight: Sarawak has the widest urban-rural internet gap in the country — 98.9% for urban households vs only 80.4% for rural households, an 18.5 percentage point divide.

03 — The Device Divide

Mobile Is Universal. Everything Else Is Not.

Mobile phone ownership is nearly universal across Malaysia at 99.5%. But computer ownership tells a different story — rural Sabah sits at just 73.9% while urban Kuala Lumpur is at 99.8%. The gap in fixed broadband is even more stark: KL has 74.3% fixed broadband penetration while Kelantan has just 25.2%.

04 — The E-Commerce Boom

Malaysia's Online Economy Crossed RM1 Trillion

E-commerce income reached RM1.23 trillion in 2024. Business-to-Business (B2B) transactions dominate at RM817.1 billion, but Business-to-Consumer (B2C) at RM336.6 billion is where everyday Malaysians are driving growth. Manufacturing and Services sectors together account for nearly all e-commerce income.

Key insight: B2C e-commerce — the kind driven by consumers shopping online — makes up 28.9% of Malaysia's total e-commerce revenue. This is the segment growing fastest as mobile internet adoption rises.

05 — The Digital Workforce

1.25 Million Malaysians Work in ICT — And Growing

The ICT industry employs 1.25 million Malaysians — 7.6% of total employment — with salaries and revenue growing every quarter. Meanwhile, 5G coverage expanded dramatically from 63.6% in 2023 to 82.0% in 2024, setting the foundation for Malaysia's next digital leap.
